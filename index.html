The Foundation

Why We Exist

Six honest reasons why this company, this founder, this moment, and this geography are worth paying attention to.

Why AI

Enterprise AI is where cloud computing was in 2008. Companies that build core infrastructure now will have compounding advantages in data, integrations, and customer trust that late entrants cannot replicate with money alone.

Why India

India produces 1.5 million engineering graduates annually. AI adoption in Indian enterprises is accelerating faster than in any other market. The cost-to-talent ratio here is unmatched globally. We do not need to relocate to build a world-class company.

Why Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu's engineering output, Zoho's success, IIT Madras's Research Park, and StartupTN's ecosystem support make Chennai a credible DeepTech hub. We are in the right geography at the right time.

Why DeepTech

Apps that wrap existing APIs have no moat. Cosmohentorq is building at the architecture level — proprietary model design, enterprise-grade AI infrastructure, and vertical-specific intelligence. Moats built here are defensible.

Why Now

Indian enterprise software sales cycles have shortened dramatically post-2023. AI procurement budgets are being allocated for the first time. The 2024–2027 window is when first-mover advantages in enterprise AI get locked in.

Why Cosmohentorq

Because someone from Tamil Nadu should do this. Cosmohentorq is proving that a world-class AI company can be built from India — not as a services company, but as a genuine product company with a global ambition.

Honest Risk Register

Things We Could Be Wrong About

Most investor decks list risks in fine print. We put ours front and centre. If any of these happen, the company may not survive. Read them seriously.

High Impact Risk

OpenAI drops enterprise prices by 80%

If OpenAI commoditises enterprise AI with aggressive pricing, the cost advantage of building locally shrinks significantly. Indian enterprises may not switch to a local provider even with better integration if the price delta disappears.

Our answer: JULIE's moat is private deployment and India-compliance, not price alone. But we could be wrong about whether enterprises care enough to pay for those.

High Impact Risk

Indian enterprise procurement cycles don't shorten

Enterprise sales in India are slow. A 12–18 month sales cycle requires capital to survive the gap. If procurement stays slow, we may run out of runway before generating meaningful revenue — even with a good product.

Our answer: We're targeting mid-market (faster decisions) before Fortune 500. But sales cycles are hard to predict and we have limited experience at commercial scale.

High Impact Risk

We can't hire a senior sales leader within budget

Technical founders often struggle to close enterprise deals at scale. A great product without sales execution doesn't generate revenue. We have not yet proven commercial distribution — this is the largest execution gap right now.

Our answer: The founder is learning sales. But this is a real risk and we are honest about it in the Founder section.

Medium Impact Risk

JULIE's architecture advantage is temporary

The AI industry moves fast. What is a genuine architectural differentiation today may become a commodity standard in 18 months. If OpenAI or Anthropic natively support on-premise deployment with JSON protocols, our enterprise moat shrinks.

Our answer: Moats evolve from architecture → data → distribution. The goal is to accumulate distribution while architecture advantage exists.

Medium Impact Risk

YAKO faces too much friction from non-tech restaurateurs

India's 7.5M restaurants are largely run by owners with low technology adoption. Getting a restaurant owner in Coimbatore to pay ₹8,000/month for AI-driven kitchen intelligence is a distribution and behaviour change problem we have not solved yet.

Our answer: YAKO will only launch after JULIE has proven revenue. It's a future bet, not a current commitment.

Medium Impact Risk

The team stays small for too long

Cosmohentorq is founder-led with a lean team. Single-founder concentration means key-person risk. If the founder is unable to perform for any reason, company operations could be severely disrupted. Team depth beyond the founder is the most honest limitation right now.

Our answer: Investor capital will in part address this. But the risk is real until the team grows.

We tell you what could go wrong because we want you to decide with full information. The companies worth backing are the ones that see their risks clearly and have a plan for each one — not the ones that bury them in a disclaimer. If these risks concern you, they should. Think carefully before participating.
